History:

The history of Futbol Club Barcelona goes from the football club's founding in 1899 and up to current time. FC Barcelona, also known simply as Barcelona and familiarly as Barça, is based in Barcelona, Catalonia, Spain. The team was founded in 1899 by a group of Swiss, English and Spanish footballers led by Joan Gamper. The club played amateur football until 1910 in various regional competitions. In 1910, the club participated in their first of many European competitions, and has since amassed fourteen UEFA trophies and a sextuple. In 1928, Barcelona co-founded La Liga, the top-tier in Spanish football, along with a string of other clubs. As of 2020, Barcelona has never been relegated from La Liga, a record they share with Athletic Bilbao and arch-rival Real Madrid.

Prominent Figures:

Joan Gamper:

On 22 October 1899, Joan Gamper placed an advertisement in Los Deportes declaring his wish to form a football club; a positive response resulted in a meeting at the Gimnasio Solé on 29 November. Eleven players attended: Walter Wild (the first director of the club), Lluís d'Ossó, Bartomeu Terradas, Otto Kunzle, Otto Maier, Enric Ducal, Pere Cabot, Josep Llobet, John Parsons and William Parsons. As a result, Football Club Barcelona was born.

The blue and red colours of the shirt were first worn in a match against Hispania in 1900; the prevailing Catalonia conception is that the colours were chosen by Joan Gamper and are those of his home team, FC Basel.

László Kubala:

Kubala signed for FC Barcelona on 15 June 1950, and as part of the deal, Ferdinand Daučík also became the Barcelona coach.

In his first La Liga season, 1951–52, Kubala scored 26 goals in 19 games. He also scored in the Copa del Rey final as Barcelona beat Valencia 4–2. This season proved to be one of the club's most successful.

Kubala missed much of the 1952–53 season after contracting tuberculosis, which threatened to end his playing career. However, he made a miraculous recovery and returned to help Barcelona retain both La Liga and the Copa del Generalísimo. He also scored again in the Copa final win, a 2–1 win over Athletic Bilbao. During his time with Barcelona, he scored a total of 14 hat-tricks.

Johan Cruyff:

Johan Cruyff joined FC Barcelona first as a player in 1973 and guided the team to its first La Liga title in 14 years including a 5-0 victory against Real Madrid in Santiago Bernabeu.

"El Salvador" earned the 'European Player of the Year' the following year.

His most memorable moment as a player of Barcelona came in a match against Atletico de Madrid when he scored an athletic goal dubbed as "Cruyff's Impossible Goal".

He came back as a coach in 1988 and revolutionized the club's style of play by implementing "Total Football" throughout all the teams including youth ranks and popularised "Tiki-Taka" style of play which defines the club to this day.

Josep "Pep" Guardiola:

Pep Guardiola is one of the first players to emerge out of La Masia into the first team and was part of Cruyff's "Dream Team".

He was such a crucial part of the team playing as a defensive midfielder that the team was built around him. He went on to captain the side.

He later came back as a coach, first for the B team, and then in 2008-09 season he coached the first team and went on to win the sextuple becoming the first team in the world to achieve the feat.

The club won 14 trophies in 4 years under his management, which is a club record, before taking a short sabbatical.

Luis Enrique:

Luis Enrique is one of the few players who have played for FC Barcelona as well as Real Madrid.

A versatile player who could play in midfield and as forward, he has appeared over 200 times for the club accumulating 73 goals. He too eventually went on to captain the team.

He coached the secondary team in 2008 before venturing in different clubs and returned to manage the first team in 2015.

The team won the treble that year becoming the first team to achieve the feat twice, with the iconic MSN (Messi, Suarez, Neymar) trio up front.

Lionel Messi

Lionel Messi is widely considered as one of the best players ever to represent the club.

Joined the academy at 13 years old he has rised through the ranks to play for the first team making his debut at the age of 17 and is currently one of the most decorated player in the club's history and the football world.

The Argentinian is the highest goal scorer and assist provider for the club.

He has won the prestigious Ballon d'Or award 6 times playing for the club which is the highest number of times an individual player has won it.

Honours:
UEFA Champions League:

FC Barcelona won its first UCL in 1992 courtesy of a powerful free kick from Ronald Koeman in the injury time.

Since then they have gone on it win it 4 more times whilst achieving the treble twice becoming one of the most succesful teams in the competition's history.

Years won - 1991/92, 2005/06, 2008/09, 2010/11, 2014/15.


La Liga:

FC Barcelona won its first La Liga title in 1929 which was the inaugural season.

The club has won 26 times which is the second highest number of times a team has won the title.

Years won - 1928/29, 1943/44, 1946/47, 1947/48, 1950/51, 1951/52, 1957/58, 1958/59, 1972/73, 1983/84, 1989/90, 1990/91, 1991/92, 1992/93, 1996/97, 1997/98, 2003/04, 2004/05, 2007/08, 2008/09, 2009/10, 2011/12, 2013/14, 2014/15, 2016/17, 2017/18.

FIFA Club World Cup:

FC Barcelona has won the title 3 times becoming one of the most succesful teams in the competition.

Years won - 2009/10, 2011/12, 2015/16.

Supercopa de Espana:

FC Barcelona has won the trophy for a record 13 times.
